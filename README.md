### About Me

Bangladesh's coastline — home to over 35 million people — faces some of the most extreme cyclone and storm surge hazards on Earth. My research focuses on understanding and modeling these threats so that communities, planners, and policymakers have better tools to prepare.

I build **high-resolution storm surge models** using **Delft3D** for the coastal regions of Bangladesh, simulating how cyclones like **Mocha (2023)** drive devastating water levels into low-lying areas. This involves multi-domain decomposition setups with nested grids (700×700+ cells), tidal boundary calibration across multiple cyclone seasons, and coupling meteorological forcing with hydrodynamic response — all aimed at producing physically realistic flood predictions.

Beyond surge modeling, I work across the full chain of coastal hazard analysis: **SAR-based flood mapping** from Sentinel-1 imagery using Google Earth Engine, **rainfall processing pipelines** for gauge-based temporal disaggregation (BMD network, 35+ stations), **cyclone track generation** from best-track datasets, and **compound hazard assessment** where surge, rainfall, and river discharge interact simultaneously.

My goal is to bridge the gap between numerical modeling research and actionable disaster risk information for the most climate-vulnerable coastal communities in the Bay of Bengal.
